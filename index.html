Issues with current designs:

A:

  • The MPH/KM/H should be in the center of the dashboard because it is genuinely the important part of a car dashboard
  • MPH/KM/H should have a control to where it only shows the users preference, as it is too clustered to have both metrics on the dashboard.

B:

  • No dashboard indicators such as seat belt, oil pressure, engine temperature, etc.
  • The gas meter is so far down on the dashboard separate from the speedometer that it is a hassle to look at and it could be hazardous as well.

C:

  • The one complaint I have with this interface is the gear display, I am not sure what 7 is, possibly overdrive? 

D:

  • The warning icons on the dashboard are confusing, such as the car icon, I am not sure what the icon is trying to convey.
  • Once again with the MPH/KM/H being displayed at the same time, it can be kind of confusing and it causes clutter.

E:

  • The oil service/inspection meter is really confusing with all the green and red lights.
  • The gas meter is confusing with the 20/30/40/70 readings, not sure if that means miles left till empty or minutes of driving left.

Common/rare uses of a car dashboard:

  • To check the speed of the vehicle.
  • To check the rpms that the vehicle is in the current gear.
  • ** check the vehicles gas level.
  • To monitor the vehicles oil pressure/engine temperature.
  • To see the current mileage/trip mileage.
  • To diagnose issues with vehicle, check engine light, etc.

Controls in interface that users interact with:

  • Resetting fuel economy readings, trip mileage, etc.
  • Display lighting, colors, etc.
  • Switching through display info such as fuel economy readings, trip mileage, etc.

Common mistakes that could be made with designs:

A:

  • User could mix up the MPH and KM/H causing them to go faster or slower than they want to go.

B:

  • Having the speedometer only in increments of 10s in not very accurate and could cause problems with adhering to speed limits.
  • Lack of information such as engine temperature could cause user to drive vehicle that is overheating, injuring the engine.

C:

  • Nothing special to not with this interface design.

D:

  • Once again, confusing MPH with KM/H could be a mistake caused by the interface.

E:

  • Once again, with the speedometer only being in increments of 10, this could lead to breaking the speed limit anywhere from 1-9 mph over.

Suggestions to improve dashboard control interface:

  • Make dashboard display different speed metrics per the user’s preference (MPH or KM/H)
  • LCD display that tells user warnings in language of choice with text to speech (low tire pressure, high engine temperature etc.)
  • Remove excessive warning symbols and only keep things such as bright light on symbol etc.
  • Display remaining miles left in gas tank as well as gas gauge with user’s distance measuring metric preference.
